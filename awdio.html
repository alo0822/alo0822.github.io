awdio

April 2015

Mobile UI Design

awdio is a white noise mobile application my friend and I started to practice mobile development.

OVERVIEW

What: A white noise mobile application

Goal: Allow users to create a personal, relaxing ambience with customizable mix of white noise soundtracks

Users: People who need white noise to relax or focus

Tools used: Photoshop, Illustrator

My Role: Created designs and mockups

INSPIRATION

My friend and I wanted to make a simple application to learn iOS development. We decided to make a white noise application, because although there are good online websites for white noise, we didn't find any mobile applications that suited our needs. There weren't any that had the types or white noise we liked, or allowed us to create our own mixes of white noise with options such as adjustable volume for each soundtrack.

APP NAME & LOGO DESIGN

To find inspiration for the app name and logo design, we came up with some adjectives that described the app and how the kind of experience we wanted to create. Some of these were calm, relax, peaceful, serenity, and chill. For the color scheme of the logo, I decided to use blue since blue is often thought to be a calming color. From the adjectives, I can up with the following three logo designs.

Logo Designs based on the ideas of sound, serenity, and chill

UI DESIGN

The UI reflects the adjectives we brainstormed and the overall experience of the app. It's a very simple design with a time display, an inspirational quote of the day, the play button, and a volume slider. The background is a darkened image to reflect the ambience created.

awdio home screen

When the user scrolls down, a list will display the soundtracks in this mix with volume sliders for each soundtrack so the user can choose which noises they want to focus on. There will also be a menu where the user can choose which mix to play, and options to add, modify, or delete their mixes.

At this point, we decided to drop this project for the time-being and focused more on Lunch Buddy instead. However, I would love to come back to this project in the future as practice for mobile UI design and iOS development.
